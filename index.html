Module 01: Overview & Introduction

Cyberbullying is a growing concern in the modern era, particularly among students in Malaysia. It is defined as the act of using digital devices, such as smartphones or computers, to harass, threaten, or humiliate another person. Unlike traditional bullying, which occurs in person, cyberbullying can happen 24/7 and reach a target anywhere they have an internet connection.

Present Continuous Tense: Used for actions happening now or current trends.
Example: "Cyberbullying is growing (trend) as a concern."
Vocabulary Tip: Use harass instead of just "annoy" to emphasize persistence.

One of the most alarming aspects of this behavior is its anonymity. Many perpetrators hide behind fake profiles, making them feel emboldened to say things they would never say in real life. This digital distance often leads to a lack of empathy for the victim.

Think About It

Have you ever seen someone being treated unkindly in a WhatsApp group? Why do you think people find it easier to be mean online?

Module 02: Causes and Effects

Vocabulary Spotlight

  • CONSEQUENCE: The result or effect of an action (often negative).
  • DEPRESSION: A clinical state of severe unhappiness and lack of hope.
  • ISOLATION: The state of being alone or separate from others.

The motives behind cyberbullying are varied. Some students engage in it for entertainment, while others seek social status or revenge. However, regardless of the cause, the effects are devastating. Victims often experience severe emotional distress, which can lead to a decline in academic performance and a loss of interest in social activities.

Long-term exposure to online harassment can result in deep psychological scars. It is essential for schools and parents to collaborate in creating a safe haven for teenagers to express their concerns without fear of judgment.

Connectors of Cause and Effect:
Use Consequently or As a result at the start of sentences to show logical outcomes.
Module 03: Digital Etiquette

Etiquette (n): The customary code of polite behavior.

"Netiquette" refers to the social conventions of online communication. Just as we use "please" and "thank you" in person, we must follow specific rules online to ensure a positive environment for everyone.
Rule 1: Think Before Posting

Once a message or image is sent, it becomes part of your digital footprint. This record is permanent and can affect your future career or education.

Rule 2: Respect Privacy

Never share someone else's private information or photos without their explicit permission. This practice, known as doxxing, is both unethical and often illegal.

VOCAB: DOXXING = publishing private info with malicious intent.
Rule 3: Tone Awareness

Text can easily be misinterpreted. Avoid using all caps (WHICH SOUNDS LIKE SHOUTING) and be careful with sarcasm.

Module 04 & 05: Interpreting & Synthesizing

Critical Reading Skills

To understand digital conflict, we must look at multiple perspectives. A "prank" to one person might be "harassment" to another. When reading online comments, ask yourself:

  • What is the author's intent?
  • Is the language biased or emotional?
  • Are there facts to support the claims being made?

Writing Prompt

"Social media companies should be legally responsible for the behavior of their users."

Modal Verbs: "Should" expresses a strong recommendation or obligation. Use "Must" for legal requirements.

How would you argue for or against this statement? Consider the balance between freedom of speech and the right to safety.

Module 06: Responsible Citizenship

BE THE CHANGE.

Digital citizenship is about more than just staying safe; it is about actively contributing to a better internet. By being an upstander instead of a bystander, you can stop the cycle of hate and support those in need.
